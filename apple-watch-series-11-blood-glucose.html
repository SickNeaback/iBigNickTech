Apple Watch Series 11: Blood Glucose Sensing Confirmed

By Nicholas Seaback from BigNickTech · June 14, 2026 · 6 min read

Apple's long-awaited health breakthrough is finally coming to the next Apple Watch. Multiple sources have confirmed that the Apple Watch Series 11 will feature non-invasive blood glucose monitoring, a feature that has been in development for over five years.

How It Works

The technology uses optical sensors and advanced algorithms to measure blood glucose levels without the need for finger pricks or continuous glucose monitors with needles. Apple has been working on this technology since at least 2021, and it appears they've finally solved the accuracy and reliability challenges.

Why This Matters

For millions of people with diabetes, this could be life-changing. No more painful finger pricks multiple times a day. No more expensive continuous glucose monitors that need to be replaced every few weeks. The Apple Watch Series 11 could provide continuous, accurate blood glucose readings with just a glance at your wrist.

Accuracy and Reliability

According to our sources, Apple has achieved accuracy levels that meet FDA requirements for non-invasive glucose monitoring. The system has been tested extensively in clinical trials, and early results are promising. However, it's important to note that this will still be marketed as a wellness feature rather than a medical device.

What Else to Expect

Beyond blood glucose monitoring, the Apple Watch Series 11 is expected to include:

  • Improved battery life (potentially up to 3 days)
  • Faster processor for better app performance
  • Enhanced health sensors
  • New watch faces optimized for health data

Release Timeline

The Apple Watch Series 11 is expected to be announced in September alongside new iPhones, with availability shortly after. Given the significance of the blood glucose feature, Apple may give it extra stage time during the announcement.

This could be the most important Apple Watch update ever, potentially changing how millions of people manage their health. Stay tuned to BigNickTech for more details as we get closer to launch.
