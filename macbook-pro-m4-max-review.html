MacBook Pro M4 Max Review: The Laptop Apple Always Promised

By Nicholas Seaback from BigNickTech · June 14, 2026 · 12 min read

After two weeks with Apple's most powerful MacBook Pro ever, one thing is abundantly clear — the M4 Max chip doesn't just push the envelope, it redesigns the envelope entirely. Final Cut renders that used to take 20 minutes now finish in under three. Battery life on a machine this powerful feels like a miracle.

The M4 Max Chip

The M4 Max represents Apple's most ambitious silicon to date. With 16 CPU cores (12 performance, 4 efficiency), 40 GPU cores, and up to 128GB of unified memory, this chip is designed for professionals who need desktop-class performance in a portable form factor.

Battery Life That Defies Physics

Despite the massive performance gains, battery life has actually improved. I'm consistently getting 14-16 hours of real-world usage, even when running demanding workloads. This is the kind of efficiency that makes you question everything you thought you knew about computing.

Display and Design

The 16-inch Liquid Retina XDR display remains one of the best in the industry, with peak brightness of 1600 nits and support for HDR content. The design is unchanged from the previous generation, but when something works this well, there's no need to fix it.

Who Should Buy This?

If you're a video editor, 3D artist, software developer, or anyone who pushes their computer to its limits, the M4 Max MacBook Pro is the tool you've been waiting for. For everyone else, the M4 Pro or even M4 models will be more than sufficient.

The MacBook Pro M4 Max isn't just an upgrade — it's a statement. Apple is showing that they can deliver professional workstation performance in a laptop that lasts all day on a single charge. This is the future of portable computing, and it's available today.
