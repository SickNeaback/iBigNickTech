Apple Tips & Tricks

Simple but effective tips to get more out of your Apple devices.

iPhone

Enable Hidden Trackpad on iPhone

Turn your keyboard into a trackpad for precise text editing on your iPhone.

How to do it:

  1. Open any app with a text field
  2. Long-press on the spacebar
  3. Move your finger to control the cursor
  4. Release to place cursor
Mac

Quick Look Files Without Opening

Preview files instantly without launching their associated applications.

How to do it:

  1. Select any file in Finder
  2. Press Spacebar
  3. Press Spacebar again to close
iPad

Use Stage Manager on iPad

Organize your apps and windows for better multitasking on iPad.

How to do it:

  1. Go to Settings > Display & Brightness
  2. Enable Stage Manager
  3. Tap the three dots in the center of your screen
  4. Drag apps to organize your workspace
Apple Watch

Unlock Your Mac with Apple Watch

Forget typing your password - let your watch handle authentication.

How to do it:

  1. On Mac: System Settings > Privacy & Security
  2. Enable "Allow your Apple Watch to unlock your Mac"
  3. Wear your watch near your Mac
  4. Mac unlocks automatically when you wake it
iPhone

Back Tap Shortcuts

Double or triple-tap the back of your iPhone to trigger actions.

How to do it:

  1. Go to Settings > Accessibility > Touch
  2. Scroll to Back Tap
  3. Choose Double Tap or Triple Tap
  4. Select an action like Screenshot, Lock Screen, or run a Shortcut
Mac

Spotlight Calculations & Conversions

Use Spotlight as a quick calculator and unit converter.

How to do it:

  1. Press Command + Space to open Spotlight
  2. Type math: "234 * 56" or "$100 in euros"
  3. Results appear instantly
  4. Press Enter to copy result
General

Text Replacement Shortcuts

Create custom keyboard shortcuts for frequently used phrases.

How to do it:

  1. Go to Settings > General > Keyboard
  2. Tap Text Replacement
  3. Tap + to add new shortcut
  4. Enter phrase and shortcut (e.g., "eml" → your@email.com)
iPad

Use Apple Pencil Hover Preview

Preview content before marking it up on supported iPad models.

How to do it:

  1. Requires Apple Pencil 2 and iPad Pro/M2 Air
  2. Hover pencil over text, shapes, or widgets
  3. See preview before marking
  4. Works in Notes, Freeform, and other apps
